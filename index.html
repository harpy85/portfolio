Field Work

Active Missions

— 6,200 m · Pacific Ocean
Project Hadal

Mapping the hadal zone of the Mariana Trench using autonomous swarm vehicles. The mission is producing the highest-resolution topographic survey of any deep-sea trench to date.

— 3,800 m · Indian Ocean
Thermal Vent Survey

Cataloguing hydrothermal vent ecosystems along the Carlsberg Ridge. Three new species of extremophile organisms have been collected for analysis since 2025.

— 1,200 m · North Atlantic
Kelp Cradle

Long-term monitoring of cold-water coral reefs off the Norwegian shelf. Sensor arrays log temperature, salinity, and biological density on six-minute cycles year-round.

Engineering

Built for pressure

  • Pressure-adaptive hull
    Ceramic-composite shells that flex rather than resist, distributing stress evenly at depths exceeding 10 km.
  • Acoustic mesh network
    Multi-node sonar arrays enable real-time data relay across a 40 km swarm diameter with sub-second latency.
  • Bio-passive lighting
    Amber-spectrum LEDs tuned to minimise fauna disturbance, paired with low-light sensors capable of 4K capture in near-total darkness.
  • 72-hour endurance cells
    Lithium-sulfur battery packs with redundant thermal management, delivering sustained operation without ascent for data transfer.
